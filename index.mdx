LSFG-Android brings the [lsfg-vk](https://github.com/PancakeTAS/lsfg-vk) Vulkan frame-generation pipeline to Android devices. It captures your game via `MediaProjection`, runs frame interpolation on-GPU using AHardwareBuffer sharing, and composites the generated frames in a transparent system overlay that sits on top of any game — no root required.

## How it works

LSFG-Android works by capturing your screen through Android's `MediaProjection` API, running frame interpolation on-GPU via the `lsfg-vk` pipeline, and compositing the result in a `SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW` (or `TYPE_ACCESSIBILITY_OVERLAY`) that floats above your game.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install and grant permissions">
    Sideload the APK, then grant overlay and screen-capture permissions. On Android 13+ you'll need to unblock Restricted Settings — the first-launch tutorial walks you through it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract shaders from your Lossless Scaling DLL">
    Pick your legitimately purchased `Lossless.dll` using the Storage Access Framework. LSFG-Android extracts the shaders on-device, stores them in private app storage, and immediately deletes the DLL copy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select target apps">
    Choose which games should trigger the overlay. The overlay arms automatically when one of your target apps comes to the foreground.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play and tune">
    Open your game. Use the draggable settings drawer to adjust multiplier, flow scale, pacing, and post-processing in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  LSFG-Android requires a legitimately purchased copy of [Lossless Scaling](https://store.steampowered.com/app/993090/Lossless_Scaling/) on Steam. The `Lossless.dll` is never distributed, downloaded, or bundled by this project.
</Note>
